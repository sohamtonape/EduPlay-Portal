Games

Choose a game to play:

  • Tic-Tac-Toe

    Play against a friend or computer in a classic 3x3 grid game. Get three in a row to win!
    Origin: Ancient game popularized in the 20th century.
    Gameplay: Two players mark spaces in a 3x3 grid aiming to align three symbols.
    Rules: Achieve three in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally to win.

  • Snake Game

    Navigate a snake to eat food and grow longer. Avoid walls and your own tail to survive and score high!
    Origin: Early video game concept, made iconic by Nokia.
    Gameplay: Control a snake to eat food and avoid colliding with walls or its own tail.
    Rules: Grow longer by eating food. Game ends on collision.

  • Rock Paper Scissors

    Challenge opponents with rock, paper, or scissors. Win by predicting and countering their moves!
    Origin: Ancient hand game popular worldwide.
    Gameplay: Players simultaneously choose rock, paper, or scissors.
    Rules: Rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, paper covers rock.

  • Typing Speed Test

    Test your typing speed with random text. Compete against time and accuracy to achieve the highest WPM!
    Origin: Typing proficiency test used since typewriters.
    Gameplay: Type a given passage as fast and accurately as possible.
    Rules: Measure words per minute (WPM) and accuracy percentage.

  • Ping Pong

    Play table tennis against the computer or a friend. Score by hitting the ball past your opponent!
    Origin: Indoor version of tennis, originated in England.
    Gameplay: Hit a ball back and forth across a table with paddles.
    Rules: Score points by forcing the opponent to fail to return the ball over the net.

  • Archery

    Aim arrows at targets to score points. Challenge yourself with increasing difficulty levels!
    Origin: Ancient skill turned competitive sport.
    Gameplay: Shoot arrows at a target for points based on accuracy.
    Rules: Score points based on arrow proximity to the center of the target.
